Chewy - The Tracker
Habit Hacker

A chat-first calorie tracker built around one idea: logging what you eat should be as easy as describing it. This is the end-to-end story.

Stack

React Native · Supabase · Node

Status

Developing - 04.26

→ Scroll through each phase
01

Discover

What's the problem?

"When friction is low, habits are easy." - James Clear, Atomic Habits

The observation

Existing calorie trackers like MyFitnessPal require searching databases, logging each ingredient separately, and navigating multiple screens just to record a meal. The friction makes consistency feel like a chore.

The inspiration

I ultimately sought a solution to my own problem - tracking macros consistently. I also admired the simplicity built in to my favourite apps like Hevy, Goodnotes (and Minecraft) and thought it could be replicated in the macro-tracking space.

Key insights

- Friends who track their macros use AI chat-bots, Google Docs and the Notes app, finding macros through online databases and a web-based searches.
- Apps with simple UI interfaces dominate as a method for tracking habitual tasks like notes apps and Hevy.

Friction kills habits Natural language is faster Simplicity drives consistency Good design is exists all around us
02

Define

Who are we designing for?

"How might we make logging meals so frictionless that it becomes a natural, automatic part of someone's day?"

Problem statement

People who want to track nutrition struggle to maintain the habit because existing apps demand too much effort per log. The barrier between intention and action is too high. The resort to oversimplistic methods then lack in accuracy and depth in historic logging.

Target user

Health-conscious individuals who have tried conventional tracking methods before.
Age demographic: 17-50
Primary use-case: Users logging individual meals within 10 seconds through a single interface, being able to reuse historic inputs and review history to understand patterns.

03

Develop

How am I building it?

Iterating, testing, iterating and testing

Core interaction

A chat-first input bar at the bottom of the screen. Users type a natural language description of their meal. The AI parses it and returns macros. One tap logs it.

Chat-bot Meal Processing Recipe Book Historic Tracking

Major Iterations

> Started with a dark theme → moved to warm light palette to emulate wellbeing essense.
> Removed Create button from main input to reduce cognitive load + Added long-press to convert logged meals to recipes.
> Merged tabs today and history into dasbboard and then dashbaord and profile to streamline progress review.

Dev decisions

Groq — AI API for free tier.
Supabase — for strong backend.
React Native — Easy deployment as app.

Design decisions

Warm paper palette instead of clinical white. Typography-led hierarchy. Progress bars instead of pie charts. Minimal tab bar with three clear destinations: Chat, Meals, Profile.

04

Deliver

What are we at now?

Three modes, one interface — Log it, Check it, or turn it into a Recipe.

Final product

A React Native app with three tabs: a chat-first food logger, a recipe library, and a profile with daily goals, meal history, calendar, and a 7-day chart. Backed by Supabase and a custom AI parsing function.
